Info about COVID

About

COVID-19 comes from a family of viruses that can cause illnesses like the common cold and other more severe respiratory illnesses like severe acute respiratory syndrome(SARS). This particular type was identified in Wuhan, China in December of 2019 and is also known coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2).

Symptoms

  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Temperature above 37C
  • Feeling feverish
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Loss of smell or taste
  • Headache
  • Abdominal pain, diarrhea, vomiting
  • Feeling very unwell

How it Spread

  1. Respiratory droplets (spread by cough or sneezes)
  2. Physical contact (touching)
  3. Touching an object with the virus on it, then touching your face

Vaccines

Pfizer Vaccine

  • Given with 2 doses, 21 days apart
  • People are not fully protected until 7 days after the second dose
  • 95% effective in preventing COVID-19

Moderna Vaccine

  • Given with 2 doses, 21 days apart
  • People not fully protected until 14 days after second dose
  • 94.1% effective in preventing COVID-19

How it Works

mRNA vaccines teach our cells to make proteins that will create an immune response without using the live virus that causes COVID-19. Once it is triggered our body then makes antibodies. If the virus does enter our body in the future these antibodies will help fight the real virus.
When given the vaccine, your cells will read the genetic instructions and produce spike protein. Then the cell displays the protein piece on its surface, and our immune system sees that that protein does not belong there and begins building an immune response and makes antibodies.

Side Effects

  • Chills
  • Feeling tired
  • Feeling feverish

How to Keep Yourself and Others Protected

  1. Try to maintain a distance of 2 meters apart form others
  2. Always wearing a mask around others + having appropriate storage and cleaning of the mask as well
  3. Try to stay away from busy, crowded and closed in areas
  4. If you must, meet people outside or in open areas with lots of ventilation
  5. Avoid touching your face
  6. Disinfect and clean common surfaces frequently (handles, phone screens, etc.)
  7. Stay home if you are feeling unwell or have any of the symptoms

Other Places in Vancouver

Basic COVID Restrictions in BC

Social Gatherings

No social gatherings of any size at your house, other than your household or core bubble.
Core bubble - usually immediate family, people who live in the same house (for people who live alone, their core bubble may contain a partner, relative and/or friend that lives in a different home)

Retail and Grocery

Accordring to PHO(Provincial Health Officer), businesses must:

  • Have capacity limits based on 5 quare metres of space per person
  • Post the occupancy limits
  • Post directional signs to have people moving in the same direction
For more Info

Restaurants

  • Must wear a mask when not at table
  • No events aloud
  • Max 6 people at a table + no moving between tables

Mask Requirments

Masks must be worn:

  • Malls, shopping centres, grocery stores, restaurants
  • Airports, public transportation
  • Places of worship
  • Libraries, community centres
  • Schools, office buildings, hospitals

Fines

Subject to $230 fine if:

  • Not wearing a mask in an indoor public setting
  • Refuse to comply with enforcement officers directions

Travel

Essential travel only

  • Usual travel for work
  • Travel for important reasons; such as medical appointments

Travel outside of BC

If coming from outside of BC

  • Must self-quarantine for 14 days
